design that works

rnbw is an environment to design the actual visual logic of your web interfaces. build websites, apps, and design systems. create with popular libraries or make your stuff. build an entire thing and use it in another thing. while you act on your ideas, HTML & CSS are changed in the background. your design (literally, your code files) is a living product with unlimited capabilities. finally, you can embrace CSS as the ultimate common ground with your development workflows. when your design is done, it’s done.

we’re not in canvas anymore

HTML and CSS are powerful design tools and happen to be the presentational programming languages of the web. essentially, they are everything you see in your browser. however, it's important to remember that when designing with these tools, you are working on something other than a canvas.

Canvas is a blank slate that you can paint with whatever colors and shapes. however, in rnbw, you’re designing for the web, so you are working within its constraints. the web is designed to display content using an adaptive structure built from boxes nested inside boxes. this allows for flexibility in moving and composing content.

design at your command

rnbw is prompt-driven and optimized for you and your keyboard. the command menus are designed to bring you that feeling of lightness while your creativity flows - toggle through your projects. add things and apply actions to them.

rnbw uses artificial intelligence to understand the structure of your project and provides you with a range of tools to manage it. You can customize the tools to your liking and use them in any way that suits your project.

optimized for openness

rnbw is open in so many ways. being open-source is part of rnbw’s journey to redefine the role of interface designers and front-front-end developers. rnbw is heavily dependent on open source - it was created to become open-source and will always be open-source.

Simple yet dangerous

rnbw is simple enough for anyone. flexible enough for anything. and open enough to use anyhow. rnbw differs from traditional vertical software by focusing on atomic concepts. while features are great "one-size-fits-all" solutions, atomic concepts multiply their meaning when combined.

by breaking down complex ideas or objects into atomic concepts, we can better understand how they work and interact with each other. rnbw allows users to build their solutions in the most vertical way possible that works for them, not others. we believe this design principle is the key to more powerful software.

files, who knew?

that’s right - you can open anything and edit it in rnbw. there are no compilers, importers, etc. you’re editing the thing right there.
